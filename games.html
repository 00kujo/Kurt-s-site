Some examples of video game genres.

Platform games traditionally started out as side scrolling. Where mainly the point is for the player to reach the end of the level. Over time these developed into 3-D models in which the character could explore an open world. Examples include: Super Mario Bros. (1985), Sonic the Hedgehog (1991), Tomb Raider (1996)

City-building games are designed to put the player in control of a city or a country to manage the populace, economics and construction. Examples include: SimCity (1989), Sid Meier's Civilization (1991), Cities: Skylines (2015)

Vehicle Simulation games are designed to provide the player with the physics and realism of operating a vehicle either by air land or sea. Examples include: Microsoft Flight Simulator 1.0 (1981), Hard Drivin (1989) 688 ATTACK SUB (1989)
